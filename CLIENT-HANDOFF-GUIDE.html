Exit Readiness Scorecard

Ownership Transfer Guide

What Is This About?

Your Exit Readiness Scorecard tool is currently running on accounts that belong to your developer. We're going to move everything to accounts that you own and control.

Think of it like this: Right now, your scorecard is like a store operating in a building someone else owns. We're moving it to a building you own, so you have full control and aren't dependent on anyone else.

What Needs to Move?

There are three things that need to be transferred to your ownership:

What It Is What It Does Plain English
The Website Files The actual scorecard application Like the blueprints and materials for a building
Website Hosting Where the scorecard lives online Like the land where your building sits
PDF Storage Where report PDFs are saved Like a filing cabinet for all the reports

Good News: These Are Already Yours

  • ActiveCampaign - Your contact/email system (no changes needed)
  • Google Analytics - Your website tracking (no changes needed)
  • Your Website - Just needs a small update to point to the new location

What You Need to Do

Important: You'll Need Some Help

This transfer involves creating accounts on technical platforms. While we've made it as simple as possible, you'll likely want to have someone with basic computer skills help you, such as:

  • A tech-savvy employee or assistant
  • Your IT person (if you have one)
  • A freelancer from Upwork or Fiverr (search for "Netlify deployment")
  • Any web developer

Estimated cost if hiring help: $50-150 for 1-2 hours of work

The Transfer Process

1
Create Your Accounts
Sign up for GitHub, Netlify, and Cloudflare using your company email. This takes about 15 minutes.
2
Copy the Website Files
Download the files from the current location and upload them to your new GitHub account.
3
Set Up PDF Storage
Create a storage space in Cloudflare for the PDF reports.
4
Connect Everything
Link your GitHub to Netlify so your website goes live. Add your ActiveCampaign and Cloudflare details.
5
Update Your Website
Change one line on your main website to point to your new scorecard location.
6
Test Everything
Complete a test assessment to make sure it all works.

Whoever helps you will need these details from you:

    Found in: ActiveCampaign → Settings → Developer → API Access
  • Your company email
    For creating the new accounts
  • Access to your main website
    To update where the scorecard is embedded

What You'll Own After Transfer

Once complete, you will have 100% ownership and control of:

  • All the website code and files
  • The hosting (where it lives online)
  • The PDF storage
  • All lead data (already in your ActiveCampaign)

You will no longer depend on your developer's accounts for anything.
